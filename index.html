I am a PhD student (entered 2015) at the University of Pennsylvania. My primary research interests are in algorithmic models of child language acquisition and how early language input interacts with the grammar. Applied, I study language change as an acquisition-driven process, as well as the insights that models of acquisition provide for natural language processing. I am advised by Charles Yang and Mitch Marcus.

From 2013 through 2015, I was an Associate Scientist at Raytheon BBN Technologies in the Speech, Language, and Multimedia group under Amit Srivastava (now ebay), Saurabh Khanwalkar (Bose), and Anoop Kumar (ISI).

In 2013, I graduated with a BA in Linguistics and a BSE in Computer and Information Science from the University of Pennsylvania.
